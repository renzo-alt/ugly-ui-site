Design Matters

Even the ugliest websites teach us something valuable about usability, readability, and structure. By analyzing them, we can better understand how to design interfaces that delight rather than frustrate.

The Ugly Website Showcase

Real examples of bad design — and how to improve them.

Example 1: Too Many Colors

This site overwhelms users with clashing colors and inconsistent fonts, making content hard to focus on.

How to Improve:

  • Use a limited, harmonious color palette.
  • Establish visual hierarchy through consistent typography.
  • Apply white space to separate content logically.

Example 2: Cluttered Layout

Tiny text, low contrast, and poor spacing make this site physically hard to read. Accessibility is ignored entirely.

How to Improve:

  • Use proper font sizes and line spacing.
  • Ensure text contrast meets accessibility standards.
  • Use visual cues and headings to guide the reader.

Example 3: Excessive Use of Low-Quality Images

This interface relies heavily on pixelated and stretched images that distract from the actual content. Combined with tiny text, low color contrast, and poor spacing, the design becomes visually tiring and difficult to navigate. Accessibility considerations appear to be completely overlooked.

How It Could Be Improved:

  • Replace low-resolution images with optimized, high-quality versions.
  • Increase font size and line spacing for better readability.
  • Ensure color contrast meets accessibility standards (WCAG AA or higher).
  • Use consistent layout spacing and visual hierarchy to guide the reader’s eye.
